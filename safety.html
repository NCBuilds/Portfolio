Workshops can be a dangerous place, to make sure everybody can work safely, please read all the safety instruction on this page very carefully. Only use tools and machines when you are totaly comfortable with it and know how to work with it on a safe way. In alle casses listen to the instructors or safety personel.


Workspace - personal protective equipment(PPE) - Clothing - Machinery - Lifting - Tools - Patience - Specific - Links

Keep the floor and workplace clear, A person can easily trip over a bag left on the floor and accidentally push into someone using a machine. This could cause a serious accident. Keep your workbench tidy. When you have finished with a tool / piece of equipment, return it to its storage cupboard / rack. A bench top, crowded with tools, will eventually lead to one or more, being knocked on to the floor, or on to feet. Tools are damaged easily and people can be injured, it also makes it easyer for others to find the tools again. Do not run / rush in the workshop. You could ‘bump’ into another person and cause an accident. You could run into a machine or bench, which could cause a serious injury.


To ensure your own protection personal protective equipment(ppe) is advised and in some casse manditory. in the workshop it is mandetory to work safety shoes with steel tows and soles, as screws and other sharp objects can be fallen on the ground, or heavy objects can fall. with the large machienes a lot of noice can be generated, hearing protection is thereby adviced when large machines are in opperation. Eye protection is also adviced to prefent injurie against flying objects. In most cases normal glasses or suficient, for thos who don't, safty glasses are adviced. Sawing wood or doeing any other opperation can lead to a lot of dust inside the workshop. we tent to keep this to a minimmum by using dust axtraction, what ALWAYS! should be used if possible, but dust/face maskes are still adviced to keep the small particles from entering your lungs.


Always wear appropriate clothing, Don's have lose clothing such as ties, cord or scarfs. This will prevent loose clothing getting caught in a machine, pulling the machine operator into the moving parts. Keep hands / hair and clothing away from moving/rotating parts of machinery. Loose Clothing and long hair, can be caught in the moving parts of a machine (e.g. the chuck of a drill). Hands / clothing should must be kept away, from the moving / rotating parts. Long hair should be tied back.


Always use guards, when operating machines. The guard on a machine, protects the user, especially the users eyes, from dangerous ‘debris’ that is thrown out, often at high speed. The guards also ensure that hands and fingers, are not near moving parts. Turn the machine off when not in use. When someone is not fimiliar with machines or has hearing protection on, somethimes they dont nowtice the machine is running, this is extremly dangerous. For this always turn off the machine. Know where the emergency stop buttons are positioned in the workshop. If you see an accident at the other side of the workshop, you can use the emergency stop button to turn off all electrical power to the machines.


when lifitng heavy objects please keep your back in mind. keep your back straigt and use your knees. A maximum of 25kg can be lifted by one person, please use the dadicated lifting machines for heavy objects. for heaver opperation a fork lift can be used, but only by certified peronel. cranes can be used but please not the maximum lifting weaght of the rope and crane. when transporting sharp objects please use gloves.


we strife for a long lasting tool life and good maintainece. but we cant see averything, so please look at it yourself before using Report any damage / faults to machines/equipment. Damage or a faulty part, could cause an accident. A broken or damaged tool can be dangerous. For example, a hammer with a loose hammer head. It is always possible that the hammer head will ‘fly off’ the handle, when it is in use.


Always be patient, never rush practical work. The most productive and efficient ‘craftspeople / engineers’, work patiently and never rush their work. Working at a safe, steady pace, is how skilled professions complete their tasks, most accedents happen cause of a rush. Never distract another person, when they are working on a machine or using tools / equipment. A distracted person could have an accident, as a distraction will take their focus and concentration away from the work they are doing. If using a tool / machine, a distraction can easily lead to an accident. this also means that you should savely approuch a persone when he is working with a machine, dont tap on thear back but try to come in eye sight.
